About me

My name is Sergio, I graduated in environmental sciences at the University of Alcalá, where I specialized in environmental microbiology. Finally I did a master's degree in bioinformatics at the European University doing my final project in the field of metagenomics and artificial intelligence applied to biomedicine.

I seek to apply my skills in bioinformatics and data analysis to contribute to the advancement of biomedical research. My goal is to use machine learning and statistical analysis techniques to identify biomarkers and detect contamination in prokaryotic gene sequences, contributing to the improvement of diagnostics and therapies. With experience in bioinformatics software development and clinical database management, I aspire to collaborate in projects that advance the understanding and treatment of diseases through cutting-edge research.

Projects

DeepClone Finder

DeepClone Finder addresses the analysis of synthetic bacterial communities to obtain an algorithm capable of estimating the relative abundance and the number of clones present for a specific gene using neural networks (Deep Learning). For this purpose, a bacterial community simulator will be developed and used to obtain predictive algorithms of different complexity.

HealthNut Study

The objective of the project is the search for prognostic and diagnostic biomarker peptides in the response to immunotherapy. For this purpose, proteomic information, IgE and IgG4 expression arrays are used. A reduction of variables was carried out since initially there were >400 predictor variables and the ideal algorithm for patient classification was selected. Finally, the hyperparameters were tuned and validated.

Microbiome

The aim of the Microbiome project was to develop an algorithm capable of classifying patients admitted for pulmonary thromboembolism according to microbiome-related variables. Initially, a permuted multivariate analysis of variance (PERMANOVA) was performed, a model similar to MANOVA, the difference being the use of distance matrices. The results obtained prompted the search for an extension of the patient database to continue with the development of the project.

SXCMX

The SXCMX project evaluates the efficacy and safety of an experimental immunotherapy treatment. My work is focused on statistical and differential expression analysis of immunoglobulins (IgE/IgG4) in peptide arrays. I am developing tools for the determination and visualization of linear epitopes.

Ovale

Ovale consists of a cohort of patients who have been followed up during immunotherapy treatment. Based on the information collected and the tests performed, the aim is to obtain an algorithm based on the regions of the protein that are recognized by the patients whose immunotherapy success has been greater.

Core Outcome Sets

The aim is to make an outcomes recommendation based on a literature search of more than 100 articles that employ a wide variety of outcomes for the evaluation of a clinical intervention. This would facilitate the extraction of information for the development of a meta-analysis. My proposal is based on clustering of outcomes using binary distances and graphical methods to establish significance, combined with data mining techniques such as association rules and the Apriori algorithm.

Note: The bioinformatics and biostatistical development of the submitted projects has been entirely carried out by me. Code cannot be published since they are under development and are confidential.
